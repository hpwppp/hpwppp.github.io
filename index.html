Post-Reflection: Key Experiences

Experience 1: Mock Interview Session

Situation
Earlier this semester, I was getting ready to apply for graduate jobs, and I was aware that my interview skills were not as strong as they should be. I had done some interviews before, but mostly in low-pressure settings, and I lacked confidence talking about myself in a professional environment. The Careers Service at the University offered a mock interview with Sharon, which seemed like a good way to practise in a realistic environment.
Task
I needed to prepare properly for the mock interview, making sure I had examples ready for common questions, especially those using the STAR method. My main aim was to present myself clearly, show genuine enthusiasm, and get honest feedback about my strengths and weaknesses. I also wanted to practise adapting to more formal, professional conversations and see how I handled nerves.
Action
I started by reading over my CV and thinking about situations where I‚Äôd shown teamwork, problem-solving, and leadership. During the mock interview, I did my best to answer each question using the STAR method and tried to keep my answers relevant and to the point. After the interview, Sharon went through what I‚Äôd done well such as keeping calm and giving real examples - and what I could improve, like not over-explaining and making sure each answer had a clear result.
Result
The session was really helpful because it showed me exactly where I was going wrong. I realised I could easily lose track of my answers or talk for too long. Sharon‚Äôs feedback was supportive, and it helped me build a plan for future improvement. I left feeling more comfortable with the interview process and more aware of how to sell myself in a fair and genuine way. I used what I had learned to prepare for my actual PwC interview, which went much better than it would have otherwise.
Learning/Planning
From this mock interview, I learned that preparation is key, and that answering questions clearly makes a big difference. It was also clear that employers really value you showing your learning and growth, not just talking about what you already know. Going forward, I will keep practising mock interviews, ask for feedback regularly, and make sure my examples are always specific and to the point. I now plan to help friends with their interviews too, as teaching others will help me remember the good habits I have developed.
